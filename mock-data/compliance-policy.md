# AeroTech Global — Export Control Compliance Policy

**Document ID:** ATG-ECP-2024-01  
**Version:** 4.2  
**Effective Date:** January 15, 2024  
**Classification:** Internal — Confidential  
**Owner:** Office of Export Compliance (OEC)

---

## Section 1: General Provisions

### §1.1 — Export License Requirement
All exports of items, software, or technical data that appear on the Commerce Control List (CCL) or United States Munitions List (USML) require a valid export license issued by the Bureau of Industry and Security (BIS) or the Directorate of Defense Trade Controls (DDTC), respectively. No shipment shall proceed without documented license approval. License number must be cited on the commercial invoice and packing list.

### §1.2 — License Exception Eligibility
Where a license exception is claimed (e.g., EAR §740), the specific exception paragraph must be cited in writing. A written self-classification memorandum justifying the exception shall be retained in the export file for a minimum of five (5) years.

### §1.3 — Recordkeeping
Per EAR Part 762, all export transaction records — including licenses, shipping documents, end-use statements, and correspondence — must be retained for a period of five (5) years from the date of export. Records must be readily retrievable upon request from BIS or DDTC.

## Section 2: Restricted Parties Screening

### §2.1 — Mandatory Screening
Prior to any export transaction, all parties to the transaction (buyer, consignee, end-user, intermediaries, and financial institutions) must be screened against:

- BIS Entity List (Supplement No. 4 to EAR Part 744)
- BIS Denied Persons List
- OFAC Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) List
- BIS Unverified List
- DDTC Debarred Parties List

### §2.2 — Screening Documentation
Results of screening checks must be printed or digitally captured and retained with the export file. A Restricted Party Screening Certificate (Form ATG-RPS-01) must be completed and signed by the Export Compliance Officer.

### §2.3 — Hit Resolution
Any potential match ("hit") against a restricted party list must be escalated to the OEC for resolution before the transaction proceeds. Under no circumstances shall a transaction proceed while a screening hit is pending resolution.

## Section 3: End-Use and End-User Controls

### §3.1 — End-Use Statement Requirement
Every export of items classified under an ECCN other than EAR99 must be accompanied by a signed End-Use Statement (Form BIS-711 or equivalent). The statement must identify the ultimate consignee, the specific end-use, and certify that the item will not be diverted to unauthorized uses.

### §3.2 — Prohibited End-Uses
No export may proceed where the exporter knows or has reason to know that the item will be used in:

- Design, development, production, or use of weapons of mass destruction
- Military end-uses in Country Group D:1 countries without specific authorization
- Any activity prohibited by EAR Part 744

### §3.3 — Deemed Export Rule
The release of controlled technology or technical data to a foreign national within the United States constitutes a "deemed export" to that person's home country. Such releases require the same authorization as a physical export. All foreign national employees, contractors, and visitors who will access controlled technical data must have a Technology Control Plan (TCP) on file and, where required, an export license.

## Section 4: Encryption Controls

### §4.1 — ECCN Classification for Encryption Items
All items, software, or components incorporating encryption functionality exceeding 64-bit symmetric key length must be classified under ECCN 5D002 or 5E002 as appropriate. Self-classification is permitted only where the item qualifies for the mass market exception under EAR §740.17.

### §4.2 — CCATS Review
Where self-classification is uncertain, a Commodity Classification Automated Tracking System (CCATS) ruling must be requested from BIS prior to export. The CCATS reference number shall be included on all export documentation.

### §4.3 — Encryption Registration
Exporters of encryption items classified under ECCN 5D002 or 5E002 must submit an encryption registration to BIS per EAR §742.15(b)(1) prior to the first export. A copy of the registration acknowledgment must be retained in the export compliance file.

### §4.4 — Country Restrictions for Encryption
Encryption items classified under ECCN 5D002 may not be exported or re-exported to Country Group E:1 (Terrorist-Supporting Countries) without a specific license. Exports to Country Group D:1 require case-by-case review.

## Section 5: ITAR and Defense Articles

### §5.1 — USML Classification
All defense articles, services, and related technical data that appear on the United States Munitions List (USML, 22 CFR §121) are subject to the International Traffic in Arms Regulations (ITAR) and require DDTC authorization prior to export. The USML category and subcategory must be identified on all export documentation.

### §5.2 — DSP-5 License Requirement
Exports of defense articles require a valid DSP-5 license or other applicable DDTC authorization. License provisos must be strictly complied with. No defense article shall be shipped without a fully executed DSP-5 or applicable exemption documented in writing.

### §5.3 — Re-export and Re-transfer
The re-export or re-transfer of US-origin defense articles to a third party or third country requires prior written authorization from DDTC. This applies regardless of whether the item has been subsequently incorporated into a foreign-made product. Re-export must be documented on Form DSP-83 or equivalent.

### §5.4 — ITAR §120 Disclosure
All export documentation and contracts for defense articles must include the mandatory ITAR disclosure statement as required by 22 CFR §120.10. The statement must specify that the item is controlled under ITAR and may not be transferred or re-exported without DDTC authorization.

## Section 6: Technology Transfer

### §6.1 — Controlled Technical Data
Technical data related to the development, production, or use of items on the CCL or USML is subject to export controls. Transmission of such data by any means — including email, cloud storage, FTP, or visual inspection — constitutes an export.

### §6.2 — Technology Control Plan
Any facility, project, or work area where foreign nationals have access to controlled technical data must implement a Technology Control Plan (TCP). The TCP must include physical and logical access controls, training requirements, and audit procedures.

### §6.3 — Fundamental Research Exclusion
The fundamental research exclusion (EAR §734.8, ITAR §120.11) applies only to basic and applied research conducted at accredited institutions of higher learning where the results are ordinarily published and shared broadly. Proprietary or restricted research, including that funded under publication-restricted contracts, does not qualify.

---

## Section 7: Voluntary Self-Disclosure

### §7.1 — Disclosure Obligation
Any known or suspected violation of export control laws or this policy must be reported to the OEC within five (5) business days of discovery. The OEC shall determine whether a Voluntary Self-Disclosure (VSD) to BIS or DDTC is warranted.

### §7.2 — Non-Retaliation
Employees who report potential violations in good faith are protected under this policy from retaliation. Reports may be made anonymously through the ATG Ethics Hotline.

---

## Section 8: Penalties

### §8.1 — Civil Penalties
Violations of the EAR may result in civil penalties of up to $300,000 per violation or twice the value of the transaction, whichever is greater. Violations of ITAR carry civil penalties up to $1,200,000 per violation.

### §8.2 — Criminal Penalties
Willful violations may result in criminal fines of up to $1,000,000 per violation and imprisonment of up to twenty (20) years for individuals. Corporations may be fined up to the greater of $10,000,000 or twice the gain or loss resulting from the offense.

### §8.3 — Collateral Consequences
Export violations may result in denial of export privileges, debarment from government contracting, and significant reputational harm.

---

## Section 9: Compliance Certifications

### §9.1 — Contract Clause Requirement
All export-related contracts, purchase orders, and licensing agreements must include the ATG Standard Export Compliance Clause (ATG-SECC-01), which incorporates by reference the applicable EAR and ITAR provisions governing the transaction.

### §9.2 — Annual Certification
All business units engaged in export activities must submit an Annual Export Compliance Certification to the OEC by January 31 of each year, attesting to compliance with this policy during the preceding calendar year.
